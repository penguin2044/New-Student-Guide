Student Accessibility Services

“RRC Polytech is committed to providing persons with documented disabilities fair and equal access to educational programs, services and facilities. Student Accessibility Services (SAS) plays a key role in this commitment by arranging accommodations and supports for students with disabilities” (RRC Polytech 2025).

Student Accessibility Services (SAS) at RRC Polytech is dedicated to supporting students with documented disabilities by ensuring fair and equal access to academic programs, services, and campus facilities. SAS works in partnership with students, faculty, and staff to provide individualized accommodations and supports that empower all learners to reach their full academic potential.

RRC Polytech is committed to creating an inclusive learning environment where every student has an equal opportunity to succeed, regardless of disability type or learning needs. Student Accessibility Services provides support for students with a range of permanent or temporary disabilities, including:

  • Physical disabilities
  • Sensory disabilities
  • Neurological disorders
  • Learning disabilities
  • Mental health conditions
  • Chronic medical conditions

Whether you're new to post-secondary studies, a returning student, or connecting with SAS for the first time, the service is here to help reduce barriers and provide guidance throughout your academic journey.

What do you gain from services?

Through Student Accessibility Services, students gain access to accommodations that promote academic success and independence. Supports may include note-taking assistance, adaptive technology, exam accommodations, and more. SAS encourages self-advocacy and helps students build the confidence and skills needed to navigate academic life with greater ease and equity.

How to access these services?

  • Visit the International Student Services
  • Visiting the Student Accessibility Services website: https://www.rrc.ca/accessibility/
  • Contacting SAS directly to discuss your eligibility and needs.
  • Booking an appointment with an Accessibility Specialist for personalized support.
  • Providing documentation of your disability to begin the accommodation process.
  • Participating in orientation sessions and accessing support resources throughout the year.

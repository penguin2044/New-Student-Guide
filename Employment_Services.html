Student Employment Services

Student Employment provides the tools and support needed to enter the job market successfully. The service also provides opportunities for students to build professional relationships that will support their long-term career growth. This service equips students with the skills and confidence needed to secure employment in their industry, helping them tailor their applications to employer expectations and improving their chances of job success. Student Employment Services at RRC Polytech, also known as Career Services, “Career Services offers a wide range of career development supports and services to current students and recent graduates.” (RRC Polytech, 2025), focuses on the following support services:

  • Career Coaching: One-on-one sessions with career advisors to help students explore career paths, develop job search strategies, and build insights and confidence.
  • Resume & Cover Letter: Provides guidance on creating professional job application documents.
  • Mock Interviews: Simulated interviews with tailored feedback to help students improve their communication and presentation skills.
  • Networking & Career Fairs: Events that allow students to engage with employers, explore industry trends, and uncover job opportunities.
  • Work-Integrated Learning (WIL): Supports students in securing co-op placements, internships, and workshop experiences to obtain hands-on work experience.
  • Further information: https://www.rrc.ca/careerservices/

What do you gain from services?

The service helps students advocate for themselves by developing persuasive resumes and interview techniques. It encourages them to clarify their career goals and explore job opportunities. By offering resume workshops and interview coaching, the service teaches students to organize their experiences and engage in professional discussions effectively. Additionally, career planning sessions assist students in setting future goals.

How to access these services?

  • Visit the Career Services website: https://www.rrc.ca/careerservices/
  • Sign up for Career Bridge: https://careerbridge.rrc.ca to access job postings and career events.
  • Book an appointment with a Career Advisor online or at a campus career office.
  • Attend career fairs, workshops, and networking events throughout the year.
